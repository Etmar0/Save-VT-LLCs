Save Living-Learning Programs

A student-led initiative to raise awareness about the Board of Visitors' recent decisions.

The Situation

On april 14 the board of visitors at Virginia tech is planning on dissolving all Living Learning Programs on campus. Furthormore they plan on only allowing first year undergraduate and transfer students to be on campus. Anyone who is not in this catagory will not be given housing on campus. This is a suden move that no students or university staff have recieved prior warning about.

Why This Matters

This resolution has the stated goal of moving more students off campus, which will raise rent prices in Blacksburg and affect EVERYONE. Furthermore, this is a direct attack on programs on campus that are considered best practices in college, that have proven highly successful, and many of which help out the most vulnerable in our community that rely on these programs (Firstgen, International, Recovery, Transfer). Students rely on these programs, and our LLPs are deciding factors for many students who just signed up for a college who is now threatening to take these programs away. The resolutions are also rampent with hipocracy stating they want to increase housing for freshman, but the board recently shut down the construction project of a multi- thousand bed "student life village" just last year.
